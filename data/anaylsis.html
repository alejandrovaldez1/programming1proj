Question 4

What are the most profitable segment customers?

In[37]:
# Use only rows with a customer number
rfm_df = df_customer.copy()

# Reference date = last date in dataset
ref_date = rfm_df['Date'].max()

rfm = (
    rfm_df.groupby('CustomerNo')
    .agg(
        Recency=('Date', lambda x: (ref_date - x.max()).days),
        Frequency=('TransactionNo', 'nunique'),
        Monetary=('Revenue', 'sum')
    )
    .reset_index()
)



# Compute quartiles
r_quartiles = rfm['Recency'].quantile([0.25, 0.5, 0.75]).to_dict()
f_quartiles = rfm['Frequency'].quantile([0.25, 0.5, 0.75]).to_dict()
m_quartiles = rfm['Monetary'].quantile([0.25, 0.5, 0.75]).to_dict()

def r_score(x):
    if x <= r_quartiles[0.25]: return 3  # most recent
    elif x <= r_quartiles[0.50]: return 2
    elif x <= r_quartiles[0.75]: return 1
    else: return 0  # least recent

def fm_score(x, quartiles):
    if x <= quartiles[0.25]: return 0
    elif x <= quartiles[0.50]: return 1
    elif x <= quartiles[0.75]: return 2
    else: return 3

rfm['R_Score'] = rfm['Recency'].apply(r_score)
rfm['F_Score'] = rfm['Frequency'].apply(lambda x: fm_score(x, f_quartiles))
rfm['M_Score'] = rfm['Monetary'].apply(lambda x: fm_score(x, m_quartiles))

rfm['RFM_Total'] = rfm[['R_Score', 'F_Score', 'M_Score']].sum(axis=1)

# Choose a threshold, e.g., top 25% of RFM_Total
threshold = rfm['RFM_Total'].quantile(0.75)
top_segment = rfm[rfm['RFM_Total'] >= threshold]

top_segment.sort_values('Monetary', ascending=False).head(10)

Question 4 Answer

I built a customer-level RFM table. The recency captures how long it has been since a customer's last purchase in days. The frequency counts how many separate transactions they made, and monetary value measures their total spending. I used quartiles to score each customer based on these three dimensions and added the scores into an overall RFM score. Customers in the top quartile of the added score are treated as the "most profitable customers" as they buy often, spend more, and typically purchase frequently. 1,267 customers fall into this most profitable group, and they account for 73% of the companys total revenue.

Question 5

Based on your findings, what strategy could you recommend?

Question 5 Answer (Summary)

Sales Trend:

Monthly revenue shows steady growth with a clear spike in November, likely from early christmas shopping. This shows that the demand is seasonal, and the business should aim to plan inventory and marketing around these spike points.

Product Mix:

The top selling products are Popcorn Holders, World War 2 Gliders Asstd Designs, and Jumbo Bag Red Retrospot, in that order. Since these 3 items account for a large share of the total units sold, the company should try to keep these products constantly in stock. They can also potentially feature them in promotions or bundles, which could further increase the amount of these products sold

Basket Size:

Customers tend to buy an average of 23 products per transaction (likely lower per person as results are skewed from outliers). The company should try to introduce selling recommendations, which are the "Customers also bought" messages. They can also try to include discounts for buying multiple items, which will influence the customers to buy more to save more.

High Value Customers

The RFM analysis displays that a relatively small amount of customers (the high value customers) contribute to a whopping 73% of the companies total revenue. The company could try to target these customers with loyalty programs, early access to products, or special offers to maintain their loyalty, ensuring they will be a customer that comes back.

After going over all of these insights, I would recommend to the company:

1. Seasonal Campaigns that align with the months with high sales

2. Shift inventory and pricing focus on the top selling products

3. Cross-selling and bundling to encourage customers to buy more

4. Loyalty/membership programs focused on the most profitable customer group

These changes can allow the company to use the observed sales patterns and customer behavior to their advantage, potentially increasing both short-term revenue (via higher basket sizes and seasonal campaigns/discounts) and long term profit (through better customer retention and optimized product focus).
